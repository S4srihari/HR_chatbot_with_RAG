EMPLOYEE RESIGNATION POLICY

PURPOSE
This policy defines the process, notice period, responsibilities, and guidelines 
for employee resignations to ensure a smooth and professional transition.

SCOPE
This policy applies to all full-time and part-time employees of the organization 
unless otherwise stated in the employment agreement.

NOTICE PERIOD

3.1 Standard Notice
Employees are required to provide a written resignation with a minimum notice period of:
X days for probationary employees
X days for confirmed employees
X days for senior/management roles

3.2 Notice Period Buyout
The company may allow buyout of the notice period at its discretion
Buyout amount equals salary for the unserved notice period

3.3 Waiver / Early Release
Management may waive or reduce the notice period based on business needs
Approval must be obtained in writing

RESIGNATION PROCESS

Resignation must be submitted in writing via email or HR system
The last working day will be calculated based on the notice period
Manager and HR will acknowledge and confirm acceptance
Exit formalities must be completed before the last working day

HANDOVER RESPONSIBILITIES

Employees must:
Complete proper handover of duties
Share documentation, access, and status of work
Return all company assets
Ensure knowledge transfer to assigned team members

EXIT INTERVIEW

HR will conduct an exit interview to gather feedback
Participation is encouraged to improve workplace practices

FINAL SETTLEMENT

Full and final settlement will be processed within X days of the last working day
Includes salary, unused leave (if applicable), and deductions
Any pending dues or asset non-return may delay settlement

CONFIDENTIALITY AND OBLIGATIONS

Employees must continue to honor confidentiality and 
non-disclosure agreements after resignation
Any conflict of interest must be disclosed

ABSCONDING

Employees who leave without notice or approval may be marked as absconding
This may impact final settlement and experience letters

POLICY REVIEW

Management reserves the right to amend this policy at any time as
per organizational or legal requirements.
